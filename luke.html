Luke 16: 10

Class : 3

“One who is faithful in a very little is also faithful in much”-Luke 16:10

LUKE 16:10 Whoever can be trusted with very little can also be trusted with much, and whoever is dishonest with very little will also be dishonest with much.

This bible verse talks about “ trust “ and “ dishonesty”. To give us a deeper understanding about “trust” and “dishonesty”, Jesus has explained these verses with a help of parable .The Bible has recorded similar verses with parables in two other places, totally three. They are

• Luke 16:10 10"Whoever can be trusted with very little can also be trusted with much, and whoever is dishonest with very little will also be dishonest with much.
• Luke 19:17 17"'Well done, my good servant!' his master replied. 'Because you have been trustworthy in a very small matter, take charge of ten cities.'
• Mathew 25:21 “His master replied, ‘Well done, good and faithful servant! You have been faithful with a few things; I will put you in charge of many things.

All these three verses are explained within parable, they are

• The parable of shrewd manager-Luke 16 :10
• The parable of Ten minas- Luke 19: 17
• The parable of the Talents –Matthew 25:14

The parable of shrewd manager-Luke 16 :10

1) Luke 16:1 The manager was accused of wasting his master’s possession
2) Luke 16:2 so he was asked to give an account of his management
3) Luke 16:5 he called each one of his master’s debtor
4) Luke 16:7, 1st person gets 50% discount, 2nd person gets 20% discount
5) Luke 16: 8 The master commended the dishonest manager because he had acted shrewdly.

In the above parable the manager is termed dishonest as well as shrewd, why is he called a dishonest manager, did he steal? no then what is his dishonesty? The master has entrusted a business to him but he completely mismanaged it hence accruing a loss to the business, when the master asked an account of his management he acted shrewdly by calling all his masters debtor and writing of some of income as insolvency and hence prevented a great loss to his master, he was commended for his shrewdness.

Here his mismanagement is called as dishonesty, his master’s property was entrusted to him, but he mismanaged and accrued a loss. A business runs into loss due to two reasons

• 1) stealing
• 2) mismanagement or carelessness or negligence

The parable of the talents, Matthew 25:14

• MATTHEW 25 :14 entrusted his property to his servants
• Matthew 25:19 Master returned and settled accounts with them.
• Matthew 25: 20 master he said ‘you entrusted me with five talents. See , I have gained five more’.
• Matthew 25 :25 ‘went out and hid your talents in the ground’
• Matthew 25:30 throw that worthless servant outside, into the darkness, where there will be weeping and garnishing of teeth.

In this parable a master entrusted his property with his servants, after a time the master returns and settles accounts with his servants, the servants who have received five and ten talents have doubled their entrusted portion of property, they were vigilant in their work, so their master commended with something great.

• Leviticus 25: 2323"The land must not be sold permanently, because the land is mine and you reside in my land as foreigners and strangers.

This whole earth with its wealth and riches is the Lords property entrusted into our hands to administer and to take care, the second coming of the Lord is nothing but settling accounts of the master with his servants. Servants who have administered shrewdly will receive their reward. The Lord have entrusted with us with different job, we must have in mind that the work given to us is from the Lord rather than out higher authority hence we must fear our Lord in doing the work sincerely and vigilantly. We cannot find an excuse in negligent work. We cannot complain about the hardships we face; it is only allowed by God to strengthen us. Negligent work will attract punishment from the Lord.
Similarly, our family, secular Jobs, monthly Income, our surroundings, our natural resource, water resource, environment, birds and animals, people we meet, church are all entrusted by the Lord into our hands, we must me diligent in administering whatever little given to us. Negligence at our administration may lead to insolvency, unhappy families, disobeying children, pollution, exploitation of natural resources, uneven distribution of wealth, diseases etc.

Punishment for negligence
Why did the servant get a harsh punishment?

• Matthew 25 :25 ‘went out and hid your talents in the ground’ • Matthew 25:30 throw that worthless servant outside, into the darkness, where there will be weeping and garnishing of teeth.
Even in this secular world the punishment for gross negligence is imprisonment
• IPC Section 304 A (culpable homicide)
• IPC Section 337 & 338
• Punishment with imprisonment

Practical exercise
